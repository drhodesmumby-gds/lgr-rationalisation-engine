    LGR Engine Terminology Glossary

    Transition Concepts

    Vesting Date

    The legal date the new unitary authority comes into existence. All Tier 1 (Day 1 critical) services must be operational by this date. Contract notice deadlines are measured relative to it.

    Predecessor

    An existing council that is being merged or restructured. Its IT estate, contracts, and services are inherited by one or more successor authorities.

    Successor

    The new unitary authority created from the reorganisation. A predecessor can be a full predecessor (all systems transfer) or partial predecessor (systems are split across multiple successors).

    Perspective

    A filtered view of the estate. "All Councils/Successors" shows the full merged picture. Selecting a specific successor or council highlights its columns and filters analysis to that entity.

    Collision

    Two or more councils have IT systems serving the same LGA standard function. Collisions require rationalisation decisions — one system must be chosen, or a disaggregation strategy defined.

    Cross-tier

    Systems from councils with different tier classifications (county vs district). May represent complementary delivery across different service layers, not duplication — requires closer analysis before assuming consolidation.

    System Properties

    Portability

    • High: Open APIs, REST/GraphQL access, or native interoperability standards. Easy machine-to-machine extraction.
    • Medium: Batch extracts (CSV/XML), standard SQL database dumps, scheduled reporting modules.
    • Low: Proprietary schema, vendor-paywalled extraction, or locked-in SaaS with no bulk export. High vendor hostage risk.

    Data Layer

    • Segmented: Data is structured cleanly and can be partitioned (e.g., filtered by ward or department for disaggregation).
    • Monolithic: Data is highly entangled across domains. Cannot be easily unpicked. High risk for migrations/splits; requires dedicated ETL planning.

    Cloud / On-Premise

    Cloud systems are externally hosted SaaS or IaaS. On-premise systems are hosted on physical council servers and may have different migration, licensing, and support implications.

    ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning)

    Monolithic business systems covering finance, HR, and payroll. Highly entangled data structures make disaggregation complex. Assessed under TCoP Point 9 (modularity).

    Shared Service

    A system jointly operated across council boundaries. Requires unwinding or re-contracting during reorganisation. May cross successor boundaries, requiring formal service unwinding plans.

    ESD/LGA Function

    Standard function from the LGA/ESD taxonomy (176 entries). Maps council-specific service names to a common classification, enabling cross-council comparison in the matrix.

    Analysis Signals

    Contract Urgency

    Classifies each system's contract end date relative to the vesting date into zones: Pre-vesting (red), Year 1 (amber), Natural expiry (blue), Long-tail (black). Accounts for notice period to identify the true action deadline.

    Notice Period Action Zone

    Contract expiry dates are misleading. If a contract has a 12-month notice period, the true "drop-dead" decision date is 12 months before expiry. The timeline visualises this as a red striped warning zone.

    User Volume

    Relative scale of systems by reported user count. Proportionality scoring identifies when one system is significantly larger than others (anchor threshold: 50% larger than the next).

    Vendor Density

    Number of distinct vendors providing systems for the same function across councils. When the same vendor appears across multiple predecessors, there is a commercial opportunity to consolidate into a single enterprise agreement.

    Tech Debt (On-premise)

    Systems hosted on council servers rather than cloud/SaaS. On-premise systems may face hosting, support, and licensing complications during transitions.

    TCoP Alignment

    Assessment against the UK Government Technology Code of Practice. Points 3 (spend), 4 (open standards), 5 (cloud first), 9 (modularity), and 11 (commercial) are evaluated from system properties.

    Rationalisation Patterns

    inherit-as-is

    Single system, no competition. The system continues under the new authority. Priority: confirm contract novation, verify licence, and confirm no data partitioning is needed.

    choose-and-consolidate

    Multiple systems serve the same function within a successor. A selection decision is required: one system is retained and others decommissioned. User volume, cost, and portability guide the choice.

    extract-and-partition

    A system crosses successor boundaries and must be split. Data and/or service must be partitioned so each successor can operate independently. Highest risk for monolithic systems.

    extract-partition-and-consolidate

    The most complex pattern: systems must be both split across successors and consolidated within each successor. Requires sequenced delivery — partition first, then consolidate within each new authority.

    Governance & Compliance

    TCoP (Technology Code of Practice)

    UK Government's framework for technology decisions. Five points are assessed: Point 3 (spend controls / vendor lock-in), Point 4 (open standards / portability), Point 5 (cloud first), Point 9 (modularity / avoid monoliths), Point 11 (commercial / contract flexibility).

    Tier Classification

    • Tier 1 (Day 1 Critical): Statutory and safeguarding services that must be operational from vesting day.
    • Tier 2 (High Priority): Important services needed post-transition but not Day 1 critical.
    • Tier 3 (Post-Day 1): Services that can be transitioned after the new authority is established. Tier 3 is promoted to Tier 2 when a contract notice trigger falls before the vesting date.

    Anchor System

    Declared when a system is at least 50% larger than the second-largest system by user count. Marginal differences will not trigger an anchor. An anchor system is generally the most logical migration target to minimise business disruption.

    Playbook Tier Mapping

    Default ESD function-to-tier classification based on MHCLG Playbook prioritisation guidance. Functions not listed default to Tier 2. Function-level overrides and Tier 3 promotion (when a contract expires before vesting) may adjust the effective tier at runtime.
